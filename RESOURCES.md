# RESOURCES

## 1. Purpose

Resources are the foundations that support civilization.

A living civilization does not only extract resources.

It manages relationships between:

- Human needs
- Technology
- Ecosystems
- Future generations

---

## 2. Resource Categories

### Physical Resources

Includes:

- Water
- Food
- Materials
- Land
- Minerals

---

### Energy Resources

Energy enables civilization activity.

Important considerations:

- Availability
- Efficiency
- Sustainability
- Environmental impact

---

### Biological Resources

Living systems provide:

- Food
- Medicine
- Ecosystem stability
- Biodiversity

---

### Information Resources

Information becomes a civilization-level resource.

Includes:

- Knowledge
- Data
- Research
- Cultural memory

---

## 3. Resource Flow

A healthy civilization requires:

Input → Transformation → Use → Recovery

Instead of:

Extraction → Consumption → Waste

---

## 4. Circular Civilization

Resources should move through cycles.

Examples:

- Recycling materials
- Restoring ecosystems
- Reusing knowledge
- Reducing unnecessary loss

---

## 5. Human-Digital Resource Coordination

Digital intelligence can improve:

- Resource monitoring
- Optimization
- Prediction
- Distribution

But decisions must consider:

- Human values
- Ecological limits
- Social fairness

---

## 6. Resource Measurement

Possible indicators:

- Efficiency
- Regeneration rate
- Waste reduction
- Accessibility
- Ecosystem health

---

## 7. Final Principle

Resources are not merely objects to consume.

They are relationships that sustain civilization.

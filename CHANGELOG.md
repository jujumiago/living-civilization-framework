This document summarizes the public development history of the Living Civilization Framework (LCF).

The framework is intended to evolve over time through research, discussion, practical experience, and community contributions.

---

# v0.3

### Added

- Responsible Capability phase (Phase 2.5)
- Demonstration phase (Phase 3.5)
- Recognition and Knowledge Sharing phase (Phase 4.5)
- Expanded education philosophy emphasizing responsibility alongside capability
- Recognition of the dignity of all honest forms of contribution
- Recognition philosophy distinguishing achievement from human worth
- Competition principles emphasizing learning, innovation, and peaceful excellence
- Updated governance concepts reflecting responsibility and mutual respect
- Expanded measurement concepts for responsible capability and contribution
- Improved legal and public-facing documentation
- Standardized disclaimers across framework documents
- AI assistance disclosure and documentation improvements

# v0.1

### Initial Release

Established the first public structure of the Living Civilization Framework, including:

- Vision
- Principles
- Roadmap
- Governance concepts

This version introduced the core development pathway:

```text
Survival
    ↓
Capability
    ↓
Contribution
    ↓
Growth
    ↓
Exploration
    ↓
Legacy
```

and established LCF as an open, evolving framework for discussion and research.

---

## Versioning Philosophy

LCF follows an incremental versioning approach.

New versions may:

- Clarify concepts
- Improve documentation
- Incorporate research
- Respond to constructive feedback
- Expand the framework while maintaining conceptual consistency

Version numbers indicate the evolution of the framework rather than completion.

The framework is expected to continue evolving as knowledge, experience, and collaboration grow.
